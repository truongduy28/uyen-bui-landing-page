This school project focuses on stimulating creativity and becoming familiar with a variation of prototyping techniques.

Timeline: May 2021 - June 2022

Paper Prototyping

Introduction

This first assignment is to create a quick paper prototype for a fitness tracker application for small devices, which is a smart phone and a smart watch. The application is at minimum able to:
• Start and stop tracking in response to user’s input
• Track the burned calories, traveled distance, and current speed
• Display this information in real-time to the user
• Display tracked data per day/week/month

How the prototypes evaluated

I sent the picture of my prototypes to my classmate Vincent and ask him for feedback. Because I am the one who knows my product best, I also tried my own design by clicking some buttons to visualize how it would work. As a result, I chose to modify the entire design for a better version. That's how I came up with the final concept.

The findings

• I should make the prototype more organized and clear
• I should include buttons or links to connect to other devices
• The prototypes still lack of basic required features like stop tracking for smartwatch or display information in real-time to the user
• I should add other features

Wireframes and Usability Testing

Introduction

For this assignment, I chose to re-design the sign-up process of Lotte Cinema Vietnam app, which I usually use for ordering movie ticket online. I added new feature like waiting screen to spice up the sign-up process, I also re-design register form to keep it easy simple and easy for users.

Wireframe

Welcome screen:

Original

Improved

In the original version, the sign-up process is designed in an unintuitive way. On the Log in page, the register panel should be prominent. In addition, the word “Join” is not clear, customers have to spend an extra second deciding which one to click on to register. I added a welcome screen for improved wireframe which does not have in the original version. I also added the writing “Best quality service with best screen” to convey the value of this application. It is clearly shows users where to Log in and Sign up.

Sign-up process:

Original
Improved

In the original version, it requries many steps to your login process. It will turn customers off and they might move on to a competing site. It is even does not have English version for international customer. Moreover, this app has a separate login page on a different website, when there could be just one simple login that takes you to the right place.
I aimed for a quick one step sign up process for the improved version. Users only needs to fill basic information. I also add a Thank you screen to show how thankful Lotte Cinema is to have user as their customer and an compelling message about membership benefits.
 

Waiting screen:

The orginal version does not have waiting screens. Therefore, I added wating screens to spice up the sign up process. I decided to show users benefits of being a Lotte Cinema’s member, it helps to bulid customer’s loyalty.

How I tested the improved wireframe:

I tested the prototype with my classmates. I sent them the link to the prototype and let them freely explore it so the mistakes are found easier instead of introducing it myself. At the end of the test,
I asked them what they thought about that sign-up process and what needs to be improved.

Results:

• All of them said the sign-up form is simple and easy to fill in
• It provides some real value like benefits of being a Lotte Cinema’s member
• There’s too much white space between “Confirm password” and “Sign up” button on the “Sign up” screen



 

Change after testing

To reduce the white space between “Confirm password” and “Sign up” button on the “Sign up” screen I decided to add a check box with the writing “I agree to Terms and Conditions”.

High-fidelity Prototype

Introduction

In this week, I built an interactive high-fidelity prototype of Lotte Cinema app sign-up process based on the wireframe of Week 3 assignment. The high-fidelity prototype is highly functional and includes most of the necessary design assets and components developed and integrated. For this assignment I used Figma. An excellent tool for making high-fi prototypes, since it provides users with a very versatile environment in which to build everything they can imagine. It lets you to enter variables, for example, so that various elements act the way you want them to.

Link to the clickable prototype:

https://www.figma.com/proto/NRgpZPxXOIySIiFR6CA6mJ/Untitled?page-id=0%3A1&node-id=1%3A6&viewport=-368%2C347%2C0.5253965258598328&scaling=scale-down

 

When the user first encountered the app, the welcome screen is a great way to convey the value of Lotte Cinema’s band “Best quality service with the best screen”. If the user hasn’t had a user account yet, they could click on the Signup button to create a new account. The next screen is the signup screen, which user has to fill in their first name, last name, email, and password. By clicking on the check box, they agree with the “Terms and Conditions”. The user could hover over the “Sign up” button to create an account. After creating an account successfully, the “Thank you screen” will show up with a smile icon. The design delivers emotional that gives rise to positive emotions. It shows how grateful Lotte Cinema to have users as its member and convey a message about the advantages of membership.

After that, user led to a waiting screen, where they need wait around 10 seconds for the process to be completed. During waiting, they could explore the benefits of being a Lotte Cinema’s member such as Point Rewards, Birthday Gift or Exclusive Promotions.

How I tested the improved wireframe:

I tested the high-fidelity protoype with my classmates: Daria, Vincent and Robin. I also watch what they're doing and how they engage with my prototype during the test. The test is conducted with only one person at a time, and I ask the participant to share the screen with me as they check out the prototype, and I take notes while they do so. After that, I asked them to express their thoughts, as well as what they liked and what they thought might be improved.

Final reflection

Throughout the course, I've used a variety of prototype tools to create various types of prototypes.

• In the begining, the goal was to create a Lo-fi Prototype of a fitness tracker for a mobile phone and watch using pencils, pens, and paper. This sort of prototype, in my opinion, is really simple to create, since it is low-cost, low-commitment, and allows you to rapidly visualize and test numerous concepts in a short amount of time. However, I find it difficult to arrange a large number of elements on a single sheet of paper; it becomes too dense or disorganized, etc., and I have to do it again and over again.

• Starting with Wireframes was a good place for me to start. For this, I choose to use the Balsamiq program. As previously stated, I thought the program was really nice, and I would suggest it to anyone who wants to use it and create a wireframe because of its convenience. Making a wireframe for the Hi-fi prototype can assist me in completing the task that I was unable to do with the paper prototype: ordering all of the app's parts. After that, I decided to work on an interactive high-fidelity prototype with Figma.

• For Exploratory Prototype , I figured out how to make a basic yet effective prototype to use in Usability Hub's A/B testing and prove my hypothesis about Color topic.

• The Wizard of Oz method seemed strange at first, but after applying it to construct the Exploratory Prototype, I was delighted. Developers utilize role-playing to evaluate how end users will interact with the prototype after it has been developed. This is a useful approach to test a prototype without having to build a complicated product and yet get feedback from users.
