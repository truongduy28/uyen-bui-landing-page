Families with young children Ethnography Research

Unilever, a big multinational, is interested in expanding their business domain. They want to develop new products and services, with a digital component, for new markets.
The design department from Unilever is asking you to dive into a subculture and to look for design inspiration and possibly new design directions to develop innovative products.

They want to know what people in the chosen subculture feel, experience and need. And what are possible design directions for new innovations that could cater to the subculture.

Role: Researcher Timeline: Dec 2021 - Jan 2022

Desk research:

As far as I have found, young child means a child below the 12 years of age. Through a blog post on Children's Bureau (2017), I also found that the importance of family in the development of children cannot be overstated, including eating behaviors. Parents and others have an impact on children's eating preferences and openness to try new foods. In addition, according to researchers, ultra-processed foods contributed 67 percent of calories ingested by children and adolescents in the U.S. in 2018 (Nunez,2021) . To summarize, not all of my beliefs about families with young children are correct. As an illustration, parents believed themselves to be the most responsible for addressing obesity in children, despite their lack of knowledge about the subject (Vittrup & McClure, 2018). Many parents and children were overweight, according to the research, however most parents mislabeled their overweight and obese children as being of normal weight. Healthy eating, portion sizes, physical activity guidelines, obesity trends, and health hazards were all areas where parents expressed uncertainty and a lack of information.
I feel that by reading the blog and the newspaper article mentioned above, I will have a better understanding of the subject and gain further background knowledge. Finally, I picked the scholarly article because it allows a researcher to speak about his or her study with other scholars in the area. They are frequently peer-reviewed by experts in the subject to verify that the content is current and correct. By integrating all three, I am able to evaluate this subculture from a variety of perspectives.

Relevance:
For the grocery store, I think it's a good opportunity to observe a big number of parents at once and see how they select ultra-processed foods and beverages for their children.

Observation style:
My observation style is` non-participant and overt observation. Because I want to pay close attention to what they're going to do and how they're going to choose ultra-foods and beverages.

Resources:

Observation protocol

I will come to the grocery store when it’s in peaked time to see as many parents with their kids as I can. I am going to use use a topic list to check all the things I want to observe. I will stand in the halls that sell ultra-processed foods so I can observe which ultra-foods or beverages parents usually purchase for their children, how they go about selecting ultra-foods or beverages for their children, and what parents seek in a meal's ingredients.

Methodological learnings

In general, I believe I have gained a better understanding of families with young children. I succeed to know frozen pizza, packaged snacks, and soft drinks are the most popular ultra-processed foods items among families with young kids. Only a few parents looked at nutrition on the package before deciding to buy a food product for their kids. Most of them looked at the food brand and put it right away in their basket. Parents were much more likely to view the information at the top of the Nutrition Facts panel (calories and fat, for instance) than the components at the bottom (vitamins and minerals). However, it’s not easy to approach parents to ask for their permission to take a picture of them and their process to choose ultra-foods or beverages for their children. Only a few of them are happy to help me to conduct my observation.

Semi-structured interview protocol

Before the interview:

Good morning! Welcome to today's interview. First, let me introduce myself. My name is Uyen, and I'm a student at The Hague University of Applied Sciences. I'd like to express my appreciation to you for taking the time to participate in this 30-minute interview. Today, I am going to ask you some questions on a subculture on which I'm conducting research: Families with young children. Your basic personal information will be written in the journal, your information and data will be kept fully private from anyone who has no relation to the research. I will not share it with anybody else except my tutor. Please inform me right away if you encounter any discomfort throughout the interview.

During the interview:
Let’s start with the first question! Can you introduce yourself?
Tell me what you know about ultra-processed foods and beverages.
Tell me about the first time your child consumed ultra-processed foods and beverages.
How often do you buy ultra-processed foods and beverages for your kids?
How do you feel about ultra-processed food and drink products for kids now available on the market
What are criteria to pick ultra-processed foods and drinks for your kids

Do you read the nutrition ingredients on the package?

This is the last question! How do you educate kids on the impact of ultra-processed foods and drinks?

After the interview:
Thank you again for taking part in this interview. I have gathered a lot of useful information; please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions. I will not share your personal information to anyone, and the data will be utilized in the course "Research for Design."

Ethnographic interview protocol

Before interview:
Hello! My name is Uyen, and I'm from The Hague University of Applied Sciences. I'm currently studying in the User Experience Design program. The reason I'm doing this interview is because it is part of the assessment for this course, which is called "Research in Design”. I'm conducting research on a subculture called "Families with Children." My questions will be regarding Families with young children. This interview is going to last for 30 minutes and your data will be kept 100% confidential from those who are not involved in the research. If you have any questions during or after the interview, please let me know immediately. We are about to start, are you ready?

During interview:
Let’s start with the first question! Could you tell me about the eating routine of your kids during the pandemic?
How did COVID impact your kids’ food behavior and consumption?
What did you do to support nutrition for your kids during the pandemic?
Tell me about the difficult challenges you faced for changing eat habits in your children during the pandemic. After that, I'll ask follow-up questions depending on her answer.
How did you overcome those difficulties? More follow-up questions to learn more about the solutions, as well as some insights into how she chose meals and drinks for her children throughout the pandemic.

After interview:
Thank you very much for sharing that interesting information with me today. I really appreciate them. I learned more about your kids’ diet, the difficulties and solutions to changing the eating habits of children during the pandemic throughout this interview. It is beneficial to my research. I'd like to tell you that I'll be using the information from this interview and the record in my research journal for academic purposes exclusively. And I'm not trying to inform anyone but my lecturer about it. I wish you a great day and goodbye!
